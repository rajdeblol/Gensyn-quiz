10 key facts about Pharos Network's modular parallel L1 blockchain

1. What is Pharos Network?

Pharos is a high-performance, EVM-compatible Layer 1 blockchain designed to unify Web2 and Web3 liquidity for real-world assets (RWAs) and decentralized finance (DeFi) at internet scale.

2. What makes Pharos ultra-fast?

It achieves 50,000+ TPS with sub-second finality through a deep-parallel architecture, reducing storage needs by 80% via GPU-inspired design.

3. What is the modular structure of Pharos?

Pharos is built as a full-stack parallel L1 with three layers: L1-Base (high-performance data availability with hardware acceleration), L1-Core (decentralized high-throughput blockchain), and L1-Extension (customizable for apps).

4. Who founded Pharos?

Founded by ex-blockchain leaders from Ant Financial and Alibaba, including CEO Alex Zhang (former CTO of Ant Chain) and CTO Meng Wu (Chief Security Officer for Ant Financial's Web3 division).

5. What are Special Processing Networks (SPNs)?

SPNs leverage heterogeneous computation for custom networks, supporting sidechains, HFT, ZKML, AI models, and more, integrated with the main chain for scalability.

6. How does Pharos support restaking?

Via the Adaptive Restaking Interaction Protocol, it integrates with protocols like Babylon and Eigenlayer for shared security and liquidity using assets like stBTC and stETH.

7. What funding has Pharos raised?

$8M in seed funding from investors including Faction, Hack VC, SN7, and Reforge, fueling development for RWAs and enterprise DeFi.

8. What is the PHRS token?

PHRS is the native gas token for transactions, governance, and ecosystem utilities, powering the Pharos DeFi and RWA economy.

9. What is the Pharos roadmap?

Testnet launched in Q1 2025, mainnet in H2 2025, with ongoing Builder Base Camp for developers building RWA apps and DeFi innovations.

10. What consensus models does Pharos use?

Supports multiple models like PBFT, PoS, and PoA for robust, scalable consensus across SPNs and the main network.
